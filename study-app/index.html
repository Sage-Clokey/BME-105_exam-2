Key Formulas & Concepts

Quick-reference for quantitative topics on Exam 2.

Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

p² + 2pq + q² = 1
p + q = 1
p = freq of dominant allele (A), q = freq of recessive allele (a). p² = AA, 2pq = Aa, q² = aa. Assumes: random mating, no mutation, no gene flow, no selection, infinite pop size.

Chi-Squared Test (HWE)

χ² = Σ (O − E)² / E
Compare observed genotype counts to expected (from HWE). df = (# genotype classes − 1). If χ² > critical value → reject HWE.

Recombination Frequency

RF = recombinant offspring / total offspring
1 cM ≈ 1% recombination
RF is the limiting factor for fine-scale mapping. Unlinked loci have RF = 50%.

Relative Fitness (Selection)

W = 1 / (1 − s)
w̄ = p²W_AA + 2pqW_Aa + q²W_aa
s = selection coefficient (0–1). Fitness values normalized to most-fit genotype. w̄ = mean population fitness.

dN/dS Ratio

dN/dS = ratio of non-syn to syn mutations
dN/dS < 1 → purifying/stabilizing selection (conserved). dN/dS = 1 → neutral. dN/dS > 1 → positive selection. Used to detect selected vs. neutral sites.

Allele Frequency Change (Selection)

Δq ≈ −sq²(1−q) / w̄
Change in recessive allele frequency per generation under selection. Recessive alleles "hide" in heterozygotes → exponential decay of q² (aa frequency).

Complementation Test

Cross: m₁/+ × m₂/+
If offspring show mutant phenotype → m₁ and m₂ are in the same gene (fail to complement). If offspring are WT → different genes (complementation). Tests whether two mutations are alleles of the same gene.

GWAS / QTL Mapping

association p-value threshold: ~5×10⁻⁸
GWAS: coarse mapping via SNP association in natural populations (high linkage). QTL: fine-scale mapping in crosses (more recombination). Associated SNP may not be causal — it's in a linked haplotype block.

Bisulfite Sequencing

unmethylated C → U (reads as T)
methylated C → C (unchanged)
Used to detect DNA methylation genome-wide. Compare to reference to identify methylated cytosines at single-base resolution.

Guide RNA (CRISPR)

crRNA: ~20 nt complementary to target
PAM: NGG (Cas9)
Guide RNA must match target DNA sequence immediately upstream of a PAM site. Cas9 cuts ~3 bp upstream of PAM. Off-target cutting is minimized by specificity of 20 nt match.

Molecular Clock

divergence time = mutations / rate
Neutral mutations accumulate at a roughly constant rate. Used to date evolutionary events. Requires calibration with fossil records or known divergence events.

Genetic Drift

Effect ∝ 1 / N_e
Larger the effective population size (N_e), the smaller the drift effect. Fixation (allele goes to 100%) is more likely in small populations. Founder effect: extreme drift in a new small population.
