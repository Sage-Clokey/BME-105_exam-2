Formulas & Quantitative Reference

Every calculation across all lectures in one place.

Product Rule

P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)
For independent events occurring together. Multiply probabilities of each independent outcome.

Sum Rule

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)
For mutually exclusive outcomes. Add probabilities when any single alternative satisfies the condition.

Mendel's F2 Ratios

Monohybrid: 3:1
Dihybrid: 9:3:3:1
Testcross: 1:1
Assumes complete dominance, unlinked loci. Dihybrid AaBb×AaBb → 9 A_B_ : 3 A_bb : 3 aaB_ : 1 aabb.

Three-point Cross

d(interval) = SCO + DCO
COC = obs DCO / exp DCO
I = 1 − COC
Add single (SCO) + double crossovers (DCO) per interval. Positive interference (I>0) = one crossover suppresses nearby crossovers.

Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium

p² + 2pq + q² = 1
p + q = 1
p=freq(A), q=freq(a). p²=AA, 2pq=Aa, q²=aa.
5 assumptions: random mating · no mutation · no migration · no selection · infinite N

Chi-Squared Test (HWE)

χ² = Σ (O − E)² / E
df = genotype classes − 1 (= 1 for biallelic). χ² > critical value → reject HWE. E = HWE expected freq × N.

Recombination Frequency

RF = recombinants / total offspring
1 cM ≈ 1% recombination
Unlinked loci → RF = 50%. RF limits fine-scale mapping resolution.

Relative Fitness & Selection

W = 1 − s
w̄ = p²W_AA + 2pqW_Aa + q²W_aa
s = selection coefficient (0=neutral, 1=lethal). Normalize best genotype to W=1. w̄ = mean pop fitness.

Allele Freq Change (Selection)

Δq ≈ −sq²(1−q) / w̄
Per-generation change for recessive allele. As q→0, most copies in heterozygotes → very slow purging → exponential decay of q².

dN/dS Ratio

dN/dS < 1 → purifying
dN/dS = 1 → neutral
dN/dS > 1 → positive selection
dN = non-synonymous rate. dS = synonymous (neutral baseline) rate.

Complementation Test

m₁/+ × m₂/+
Mutant F1 → same gene (fail to complement). WT F1 → different genes (complement). Both mutations must be recessive and in trans.

Bisulfite Sequencing

unmethylated C → U → reads as T
methylated C → stays C
Compare bisulfite-treated to reference to find 5mC at single-base resolution. Nanopore/PacBio detect methylation directly from signal.

CRISPR Guide RNA

crRNA: ~20 nt + PAM (NGG)
PAM = NGG immediately 3' of protospacer on non-target strand. Cas9 cuts ~3 bp upstream. NHEJ→indels→KO. HDR+template→knockin.

GWAS Threshold

p < 5 × 10⁻⁸
Bonferroni for ~1M independent SNP tests. Hit SNP may not be causal — it's in LD with causal variant in same haplotype block.

Genetic Drift

Effect ∝ 1 / N_e
Small N_e → large drift → fast fixation/loss. Founder effect = new small population. Bottleneck = temporary N reduction.

Molecular Clock

divergence time = mutations / rate
Neutral mutations accumulate at ~constant rate. Calibrated with fossils. Gene tree ≠ species tree (ILS, HGT, gene duplication).

Biology & Political Economy

Living systems as models for political and economic principles — every major concept in BME 105 has a structural analogy in how societies organize themselves.

Every living system solves the same fundamental problems that human societies face: how to allocate scarce resources, who controls information, how to handle conflict between individual and collective interest, and what rules govern cooperation vs. exploitation. Because biology solved these problems first — over billions of years of selection — its solutions are deep structural templates for political and economic thought.

Evolutionary Guidance: Designing Biological Systems Without Central Planning
If markets and evolution are structurally identical — both decentralized, undirected, emergent — then the same critique of central planning applies to biological design. A genetic engineer who specifies every outcome is a central planner. The question is: what does it look like to guide evolution as an agent of it, rather than override it? Every tool in this course answers that question differently.
Central Planner Approach
  • ✗Specify the desired outcome directly
  • ✗Override the regulatory landscape
  • ✗Ignore epistasis and pleiotropy
  • ✗Reduce diversity to reach a fixed target
  • ✗Permanent edits before understanding context
  • ✗Trans-acting global interventions everywhere
  • ✗Design from scratch rather than modify existing
Evolutionary Guidance Approach
  • ✓Change the fitness landscape, let selection act
  • ✓Read the regulatory landscape before touching it
  • ✓Map epistasis first — know your network
  • ✓Maintain diversity — it is the raw material
  • ✓Reversible before permanent
  • ✓Cis (local) before trans (global)
  • ✓Work with existing variation, not against it
Tool (from course) Intervention Type Evolutionary Guidance Role
GWAS & QTL Mapping Observe / Map Survey existing natural variation — find what evolution has already solved. Never design from scratch what selection has already optimized. These are price signals: variants that are common and fit have survived selection pressure.
Forward Genetics (EMS screens) Observe / Map Let random mutation explore sequence space; observe what phenotypes emerge. You create the conditions (mutagen + selection), evolution does the searching. The phenotype-first approach respects that biology knows things we don't.
ATAC-seq / ChIP-seq / Hi-C Observe / Map Read the regulatory landscape before intervening. Enhancers, insulators, TADs — this is the evolved institutional structure of the genome. Acting without reading it is legislating without reading the constitution.
Reporter Genes (GFP, luciferase) Observe / Map Observe without interfering — attach a sensor to existing regulation and watch. The purest form of not being a central planner: you listen to what the system is doing.
RNAi / Gene Knockdown Reversible Modulation Temporary silencing without permanent sequence change — the equivalent of a policy trial rather than a constitutional amendment. Test the phenotypic consequence before committing. If the effect is wrong, stop the RNAi.
Epigenetic Editing (methylation) Reversible Modulation Change gene expression without changing sequence — manipulate the institutional culture without rewriting the law. Heritable within mitotic lineages but erased in meiosis. Medium-term intervention with natural exit.
Conditional KO (LoxP / Cre-ERT2) Targeted Intervention Tissue-specific and time-inducible knockout — the most precise available intervention. You don't knock out a gene everywhere forever; you remove it in one cell type, at one time, to test one hypothesis. Minimal blast radius.
Complementation Test Causal Verification Before acting, verify causality — confirm the gene actually causes the phenotype. The political equivalent: before passing a law to fix a problem, confirm the problem is actually caused by what you think.
Rescue Experiment Causal Verification Re-introduce the wild-type gene to confirm the phenotype is gene-specific, not a side effect of disruption. Analogous to a policy rollback test — can you restore the original state?
Model Organisms (C. elegans, Drosophila) Scaled Testing Test in small, fast, tractable populations before scaling to humans. This is the experimental analog of a pilot program or Special Economic Zone — try the intervention in a contained system before systemic rollout.
CRISPR-Cas9 (NHEJ knockout) Surgical Intervention Precisely remove a single gene's function. The most targeted permanent intervention available — analogous to repealing a single law rather than rewriting the entire code. Still permanent; only use after all reversible approaches are exhausted and causality is proven.
HDR Knock-in Surgical Intervention Replace a sequence with a designed template — the maximum intervention. Justified only when: (1) the causal variant is known exactly, (2) reversible methods failed, (3) the off-target risk is characterized. The constitutional amendment of the genome.
Phylogenetics & Molecular Clock Historical Context Understand evolutionary history before acting. Conserved sequences across species signal purifying selection — high constraint, dangerous to touch. Rapidly evolving regions are where selection permits more variation. Respect the dN/dS signal.
Population Genetics / HWE System Monitoring Monitor the population for HWE violations — detect when selection, drift, or other forces are actively shaping allele frequencies. This is macroeconomic monitoring: look for market failures before intervening, and verify your intervention isn't causing new ones.
eQTL Mapping Regulatory Audit Find genetic variants that change gene expression — identifies which regulatory regions are functionally variable in the population. Before editing an enhancer, check if natural variants already exist. If they do, they're the market price of that regulatory element.
Five Principles of Evolutionary Guidance
1. Observe Before Acting
Forward genetics, GWAS, ATAC-seq, reporter genes — all are listening tools. The genome has already encoded billions of years of solutions. Map what exists before proposing what should exist. Hayek's price signal problem: the regulatory genome is a distributed information system; read it before overriding it.
2. Change Conditions, Not Outcomes
The evolutionary way to get a different organism is to change the selection environment and let variation + selection do the work. QTL mapping reveals which variants are fit under which conditions. You design the fitness landscape; you don't specify the winner. This is how breeders domesticated wheat — not by engineering the genome, but by selecting over generations.
3. Reversible Before Permanent
RNAi → epigenetic editing → conditional KO → CRISPR KO → HDR knock-in. Each step is more permanent and more consequential. Never use a constitutional amendment when a policy trial will do. Epistemically, you don't know enough to be permanent yet. The Cre-ERT2 system embodies this — tamoxifen-inducible, meaning you can choose when to cross the threshold.
4. Respect Epistasis & Pleiotropy
Every gene interacts with others (epistasis) and affects multiple traits (pleiotropy). There is no such thing as an isolated intervention. The central planner's error: assuming you can change one variable while holding all others constant. Complementation tests and rescue experiments exist precisely because this assumption fails constantly. Map the network before you pull a node.
5. Preserve Diversity as Capital
Genetic diversity is the raw material of future adaptation. A bottleneck (low N, high drift) eliminates options permanently. Fixing one allele is closing a market. Balancing selection (HbA/HbS) exists because keeping multiple alleles has adaptive value that no single optimal allele captures. In biological design: maintain a diversity of approaches rather than optimizing toward a single solution.
6. Honor the Holobiont
The organism is not a closed system — it is a holobiont, a community of host + microbiome + symbionts. Intervening in "the organism" without accounting for its microbial partners is the central-planner error of treating the economy as if it had no informal institutions. The microbiome is not noise; it is co-evolved infrastructure.

Case Study: Genomic Imprinting as a Negotiated Settlement

The parental conflict hypothesis says: the paternal genome maximizes resource extraction from the mother; the maternal genome conserves resources for future offspring. Imprinting — silencing one parental allele via DNA methylation — is the evolved settlement of this conflict. IGF2 (paternal, expressed) drives fetal growth. H19 (maternal, expressed) suppresses it. The organism runs both simultaneously and reaches a phenotype neither parent would have chosen unilaterally.

This is the biological version of a constitutional compromise between competing interests. Neither party has full control. The regulatory mechanism (ICR methylation) is enforced by the system itself, not by either party. The "design" emerged from conflict, not from a plan.

Lesson for biological design: when two selection pressures conflict (growth vs. conservation, offense vs. defense, short-term vs. long-term), do not simply maximize one. Build a negotiation mechanism — a regulatory region that senses both signals and integrates them. This is what evolution did. It is what institutional design should do.

Case Study: Endosymbiosis as the Greatest Non-Planned Integration

Mitochondria are the result of an ancient mutualistic event: a proteobacterium was engulfed by an archaeon and, instead of being digested, became a permanent energy-producing partner. No designer specified this. The evidence — circular mtDNA, 70S ribosomes, double membranes, binary fission, maternal inheritance — shows these organelles are evolutionarily independent organisms that were integrated over time.

This is the most important "design" decision in the history of eukaryotic life, and it was not designed. It emerged from a mutualistic interaction that happened to be more fit than either party alone. The mitochondria eventually transferred most of its genes to the nuclear genome — a process of integration over hundreds of millions of years that no engineer would have specified.

Lesson: the best biological "design" may come from creating conditions for mutualistic integration, not from engineering a monolithic system. The holobiont strategy — host + microbiome — follows the same logic. You don't design the microbiome; you create a gut environment where beneficial microbes outcompete harmful ones.

The Spiral Steward: A Framework for Biological Design Without Domination
"There are two ways to build a world. One way begins with a ruler, a blueprint, and a grid. It assumes that order must be imposed, that coherence must be commanded, that life must be shaped by an external mind standing above it... The Block believes that complexity is chaos and must be disciplined. It extracts, standardizes, and dominates. It says: 'We will decide what is good, and everything else must conform.' For a time, the Block appears powerful. But beneath the surface, brittleness accumulates. The Block produces the illusion of order while seeding the reality of collapse. This is not merely a political mistake. It is a biological one." — Spirals Remember. Blocks Forget. (Sagent Creed)
The Mason — The Block
Turns the natural world first into blocks, then builds with them. Imposes structure from outside. Flattens hills, straightens rivers, replaces forests with plantations. Optimizes one variable and collapses the ecosystem that made it possible. Produces monocultures that look efficient but are fragile.

In biology: specifies the exact sequence, overrides regulatory networks, ignores epistasis, drives alleles to fixation, treats the organism as a machine to be engineered part by part.
The Steward — The Spiral
Utilizes the natural shape and allows the spiral in their design. Does not ask "What shall we force this place to become?" but "What wants to grow here?" Designs not finished products but possibility spaces — resilient sets of options that allow organisms and communities to adapt.

In biology: reads the regulatory landscape, changes conditions rather than outcomes, maintains diversity, uses reversible tools, lets selection do the searching. A gardener, not a ruler.
"There are the masons and the stewards. The masons turn the natural world first into blocks then they used those blocks to build. The stewards utilize the natural shape and allows the spiral in their design and designs with nature instead of against it." — Sagent Creed (original note)

Biological Structure Why It Is a Spiral, Not a Block

The Sagent: An Agent Within the Evolutionary Process

What a Sagent Is

The Sagent is not a ruler who stands above the system and commands it. The Sagent is an agent within the living order — someone who acts from conscience, reads the system deeply, and intervenes with precision and humility. The Sagent's legitimacy comes not from authority but from alignment with truth.

In biological terms: the Sagent is a biologist who has internalized that the genome is not their property to redesign, but a living covenant they are temporarily stewarding. They use tools that listen before they speak, that are reversible before permanent, that work with existing variation before introducing novel sequence. They understand that their deepest obligation is not to produce a specific outcome but to preserve the conditions under which life can continue adapting.

Conscience is Crown — before any intervention, ask: does this preserve or destroy diversity? Does this work with the system's own logic or against it? Is there a more reversible path? The researcher's conscience is the final check that no IRB replaces.

The model organism (C. elegans, Drosophila, mouse) is not a tool to be consumed but a window into evolutionary truth. The microbiome is not contamination but a co-evolved partner. The cell is not a chassis — it is an agent.

Why This Matters

Traditional genetic engineering tools operate at the level of sequences — the 1s and 0s of biology. They are the biological equivalent of programming in assembly language: technically complete, but impossible to use at the scale and complexity that real biological design requires.

What the Spiral Steward framework demands instead is abstraction layers that match how living systems actually work:

Layer 1 — Growth patterns (not static parts): design in terms of developmental trajectories, not final states. A cell does not have a blueprint; it has a program that reads environmental signals and unfolds a trajectory. Design the trajectory.

Layer 2 — Feedback mechanisms (not one-way commands): every designed circuit should include negative feedback (homeostasis) and the ability to self-correct. The gene regulatory network model from L14 is the template — enhancers, silencers, insulators, TFs all in feedback.

Layer 3 — ML for sequence-to-structure: AlphaFold proved this layer exists. The next step is a generative model that goes from desired cellular behavior → regulatory network architecture → DNA sequence. This is the compiler for the biological programming language.

Layer 4 — Evolutionary testing: the design isn't done until it has been run through a selection process — in model organisms first, then scaled. QTL mapping and experimental evolution are the beta-testing and iteration cycles. No release without running the population.
"Bioinformatics offers a different posture: attention before intervention. It reveals that life is not centrally designed, but emergent: a decentralized order created through layers of feedback, adaptation, and symbiosis. When you do computational biology, you are forced into humility. You learn that you cannot design an entire living world top-down. You can only nudge, explore, and make local improvements — then observe what the system does in response. The deeper your models become, the more obvious it becomes: life is not a block you carve into shape; it is a spiral you cultivate. Start designing like gardeners, not rulers. Not blocks. Spirals." — Bioinformatics as Stewardship of Living Order (Sagent writings)

The Deepest Structural Parallel: Evolution = Markets

The most profound analogy is between evolution by natural selection and market competition. Both are decentralized, undirected processes that produce complex adaptive order without a designer. Both reward fitness/profit ruthlessly. Both eliminate what doesn't work through blind trial and error at massive scale.

Hayek's key insight — that prices aggregate distributed information no central planner can ever possess — is the economic equivalent of natural selection aggregating fitness information across an entire population. The market is the economy's natural selection. Both process more information, faster, than any top-down authority can.

The opposing view — central planning — maps to intelligent design: a designer specifies outcomes directly. The evolutionary/market critique is identical in both cases: the designer lacks the local, distributed, real-time information and feedback speed that the decentralized process has. Complexity cannot be imposed; it must be allowed to emerge.

This is also why parasitism is only evolutionarily stable at moderate rates: a parasite that kills its host too fast destroys its own resource base. The same constraint applies to any extractive system — there is a maximum sustainable extraction rate beyond which the host (productive population, economy) collapses, taking the parasite with it.
